Air Fryer Brussels Sprouts

When the world flipped for crispy brussels sprouts, many new sprout lovers were disappointed to learn that deep fat frying was the restaurant trick for making them crispy so quickly. Deep frying at home is messy, and roasting, while cleaner and healthier for you, takes a while. But now, thanks to the air fryer, it is possible to recreate these crispy, tender brussels sprouts at home with very little effort in under 20 minutes.

  1. 1 lb. brussels sprouts, trimmed and halved
  2. 1 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  3. Kosher salt
  4. Freshly ground black pepper
  5. Pinch of crushed red pepper flakes
  6. Juice of 1/2 lemon
  7. 1 clove garlic, minced
  8. 1 tbsp. honey
  9. 1 tbsp. red wine vinegar
  10. 2 tsp. Dijon mustard

Cilantro-Lime Chicken

Cilantro and lime is a classic duo that we can't get enough of. Serve this with our cilantro lime rice if you can't either.

  1. 4 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  2. Juice of 2 limes
  3. 1/4 c. freshly chopped cilantro
  4. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  5. 1/2 tsp. cumin
  6. Pinch crushed red pepper flakes
  7. 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  8. 1Kosher salt
  9. Freshly ground black pepper
  10. Cooked white rice, for serving

Pasta Pomodoro

When it comes to an easy, fast dinner, there's nothing better than Pasta Pomodoro. Simpler to make than many traditional pasta sauces, pomodoro sauce focuses on highlighting fresh tomatoes by cooking them down just enough to become a light sauce and leaving many large chunks of tomato still intact. Enjoy this lovely fresh pasta dish topped with a mound of grated Parmesan, preferably outside, preferably with a glass of crisp white wine.

  1. 1 lb. spaghetti
  2. 2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
  3. 1 small yellow onion, chopped
  4. 3 cloves garlic, minced
  5. 4 medium tomatoes, chopped (about 4 cups)
  6. Kosher salt
  7. Freshly ground black pepper
  8. Pinch crushed red pepper flakes
  9. 2 tbsp. thinly sliced basil, plus more for serving
  10. Freshly grated Parmesan, for serving
